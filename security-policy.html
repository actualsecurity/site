Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

We take security seriously — including the security of our own infrastructure. If you've found a vulnerability, we want to hear about it.

Last updated: April 2026

Scope

This policy applies to vulnerabilities found in the following assets:

  • actualsecurity.org and all content served from the domain
  • *.actualsecurity.org — any subdomain
  • Any other web property or service explicitly operated by Actual Security

If you're unsure whether a target is in scope, ask us first at security@actualsecurity.org before testing.

How to Report

Send your findings to:

security@actualsecurity.org

For sensitive reports, encrypt your email using our PGP key.

Please use a descriptive subject line. If you don't receive a response within 48 hours, follow up — things happen.

What to Include

A good vulnerability report helps us act quickly. Please include:

  • A clear description of the vulnerability and the affected asset
  • Step-by-step instructions to reproduce the issue
  • An assessment of the potential impact (what could an attacker achieve?)
  • Any proof-of-concept code, screenshots, or logs that demonstrate the issue
  • Your suggested severity rating (Critical / High / Medium / Low / Informational)
  • Your contact information for follow-up

Safe Harbor

We believe security researchers acting in good faith should not face legal consequences for their work. If you follow this policy, we commit to:

  • Not pursuing legal action against you for your research activities
  • Not filing complaints with law enforcement regarding your findings
  • Working with you to understand and resolve the issue
  • Treating your report in confidence — we will not share your identity without permission

Good faith means you did not access, modify, or delete data belonging to others; did not degrade our services; and made a genuine effort to avoid harm during your research.

Response Timeline

We aim to handle all reports promptly. Here is what you can expect:

48 hours Initial acknowledgement of your report 5 business days Triage and initial severity assessment Every 2 weeks Status updates until the issue is resolved Resolution Notification when the vulnerability has been fixed

Timelines may vary for complex issues, but we will keep you informed.

Out of Scope

The following are not covered by this policy and should not be tested:

  • Social engineering attacks against Actual Security employees or contractors
  • Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks
  • Physical attacks against offices, data centers, or other facilities
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party services, libraries, or platforms we don't control
  • Automated scanning that generates excessive traffic or degrades service
  • Findings from software or infrastructure that is end-of-life or no longer maintained

Recognition

We believe in giving credit where it's due. With your permission, we will publicly acknowledge your contribution in our security researcher hall of fame.

You may choose to be credited by name, handle, or remain anonymous — it's entirely up to you. Let us know your preference when you submit your report.

Rewards

Actual Security does not currently operate a paid bug bounty program. We do not offer monetary compensation for vulnerability reports at this time.

That said, we genuinely appreciate the effort and skill involved in responsible disclosure. Every valid report makes our systems and our clients more secure, and we don't take that for granted.
